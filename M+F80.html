The 80s


Music stars influenced fashion trends throughout the 1980s. American singers like Madonna introduced sexy trends like visible bra straps and wearing underwear as outerwear. Michael Jackson wore a studded black leather jacket in his sucessful music video (Thriller), young people were seen sporting the same trend. Film and television had a similar impact. The movie Flashdance made leg warmers and ripped off-the-shoulder t-shirts very popular. The wide-shouldered suits from soap opera Dynasty filtered into fashion, while series Dallas promoted displays of wealth like heavy, glitzy jewlery and sparkling, sequined clothing.

Fashion in the 1980s change dramatically with the incoming new music. “The artists of the 80s found that looking good, showing some skin, having an outlandish outfit or two, and/or wearing more makeup than a circus clown helped to make a lasting impression on impressionable record buyers” (Totally Awesome 80s). You would see girls/women wearing tighter clothing, low cut shirts, big hair, bright colors, baggy pants, jeans, bright and colorful makeup, fake eyelashes, leg warmers, off the shoulder tops, and poufy dresses. The biggest fashion trend of the 80s was jeans. Women were wearing jeans now not for a political statement, but for a comfy pair of casual pants. Early in the 80s they were tight, straight legged, creased, and as blue as the night sky. Women hair was teased, crimped, and even colorful.



The 90s

The b-boy style was introduced in the 1990s baggy jeans and shorts, professional sports team clothing, baseball caps turned backwards (NWA), and chains with huge gold pendants (Run DMC and LL Cool J). Sweat/Jump suits became very popular with the runways and supermodels. Rock artist of the 90s created their own fashion statements like torn jeans, long hair, leather jackets, and headbands.

The range of 90s fashions Sincluded the khaki/black/white combo, provided by Banana Republic and The Gap. Footwear took another direction with comfortable options like Doc Martens and hiking boots. 1990s women’s fashion revealing with Clueless chunky heeled shoes, A-Line mini dresses and underskirt style short dresses worn over bodysuits or T-Shirts and music scene-inspired cut-off jeans, tiny midriff-baring tops and belly button piercings. Drew Barrymore made the foot tattoo famous in the movie Poison Ivy and the nineties fashion dilemma of whether to wear hair with banana clips, scrunchies and tiny plastic butterfly clasps or go for the Grunge side of 90s fashion with long straight center-parted hair.
